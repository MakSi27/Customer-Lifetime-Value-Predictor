# Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Predictor: Business Charter

## 1. Project Overview
The objective of this project is to build a machine learning model capable of predicting the **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** of our users based on their demographics, purchase history, and engagement behavior. CLV represents the total revenue a company can expect to generate from a customer over the entire duration of their relationship.

## 2. Core Business Questions
This project aims to answer the following strategic questions:
1. **Who are our most valuable customers?** Can we identify the specific traits (e.g., location, age, gender) of customers who generate the highest lifetime value?
2. **How does engagement impact spending?** Does having a subscription, using promo codes, or selecting express shipping significantly increase a customer's total value?
3. **Which product categories drive loyalty?** Are there specific product categories (e.g., Outerwear vs. Accessories) that lead to longer retention and higher overall spend?
4. **What is the projected value of a new customer?** Can we accurately predict a new user's lifetime spend based on their initial profile and first few purchases?

## 3. Expected End Results
* **A Predictive Model:** A trained machine learning algorithm that takes in a customer's profile and outputs an estimated dollar value for their expected lifetime spend.
* **Customer Segments:** Clear identification of "High", "Medium", and "Low" value customer cohorts.
* **Feature Importance:** A definitive breakdown of which specific behaviors (like buying weekly vs. monthly) actually mathematically drive the most revenue.

## 4. How the Results Will Be Used
* **Marketing Budget Optimization:** Shift advertising dollars toward acquiring customers whose profiles match our "High-Value" predictions.
* **Retention Strategies:** Intervene with special offers or loyalty rewards to prevent valuable customers from churning.
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Management:** Ensure we aren't spending $50 to acquire a customer whom the model predicts will only bring in $30 of lifetime value.
